## Definition

[[rayleigh-scattering]] is the scattering of light by particles much smaller than the wavelength of that light, with shorter wavelengths scattering more strongly than longer wavelengths.

## Context

This concept appears in the source because it is given as the explanation for why the sky appears blue.

## Examples

- The source uses the blue sky as an example of Rayleigh scattering.
